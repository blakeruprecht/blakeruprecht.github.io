Tools

Tools are passive agents: parts of the environment that agents can use to perform actions faster. Tools get better over time as technology improves. Technology refers to the collective cultural knowledge of how to make tools, and the existing tools that are required to make more specialized tools.

Humans perform actions on parts of the environment (resources, e.g. water, air, sunlight, sand, dirt, rocks, metals, plants e.g. trees, animals e.g. cows) in order to change what the environment looks like, creating new passive agents called tools in the process.

Building is what I call the process of making tools. I build (or buy) all of my own tools. Some people are professional builders and do that for work, but most people aren’t, but still need to build.

Building refers to the process of making tools. Technology refers to the specialized knowledge and tools required to make new tools.
