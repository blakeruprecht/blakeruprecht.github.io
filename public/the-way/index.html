Simple Fitness Requirements

In order of Homo sapiens survival need:

  • breathe - recognition that we are alive, here and now, in this present moment
  • move - moving our legs and arms and bodies in general is fundamental
  • rest - humans need sleep, hygiene, and medicine to keep moving forward
  • nourish - humans need water and food to survive
  • relate - humans need teams/groups/tribes to produce enough food to survive
  • work - humans need a continual supply of resources to keep surviving

^ You may be intimidate by my note list above. Anytime I see words I feel that too. So here’s a quick summary:

The goal of all life forms is to reproduce. Why?

Because a few tiny particles formed structures due to physical forces. These structures formed other structures. With enough variance, these structures started to exhibit properties. These blobs were the first single-celled organisms – they built walls to protect the cell, roads to distribute energy, nerves to detect sensation from the environment, cells to process the electrical signals.

These cells formed larger structures, which specialized over time.

These structures kept evolving through the process of reproduction.

These structures get more complex over time as more specialized cells are randomly reproduced because of genetic mutation.

The structures that want to make more structures and successfully do become more plentiful. The structures that don’t want to reproduce don’t. Enough structures exist that eventually most of them want to reproduce, and the some that don’t die off.

The cycle continues.

Most humans want to reproduce, and keep living. These are innate drives. Some don’t feel them.

For the humans that want to live, they accomplish this by:

  • breathing deeply
  • moving quickly
  • resting plenty
  • nourishing simply
  • relating cooperatively
  • working enough

So from first principles, each human has to decide for themselves if they want to keep living (the default answer is “Y”, literally “yes” and “why”).
